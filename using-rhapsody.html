How to Use Rhapsody

Five practices that help the Word take root.

Rhapsody of Realities brings a fresh perspective from God's Word every day — the day's topic, a theme scripture, the message, the daily confession, and the Bible reading plan. Here's how to get the most from it, in order.

01

Set aside a quiet time

The best time to study Rhapsody of Realities isn't on your way to lunch or rushing into your car. Practice reading it at the start of the day, not the middle or end. Give your study time real attention — don't study on the run; be settled, and you'll get more out of it.

02

Speak the confession out loud

Reading the confessions and prayers silently isn't the same as confessing them audibly — that's the only way they produce results in your life. One reader recited his daily confessions over and over until the words got into his spirit; the results showed up in his job, his family, and every area of his life. Don't mumble the prayers either — say them out loud, and don't be afraid to return to them later in the day.

03

Pick a Bible reading plan and stick with it

Go through the entire Bible in one year with the one-year reading plan, or in two years with the two-year plan — whichever you choose, consistency is what makes it work.

04

Follow the counsel in each article

While studying, you'll often find tips, instructions, and insights woven into the message. Take heed and follow them — they're spiritual instructions that can move you to a higher level when observed.

05

Have faith, and watch your life transformed

Always expect to get more from Rhapsody of Realities — there's more in it than you're currently getting. Approach it with that expectation, every day.
